About JPAD

JPAD is a Java-Powered Alternative Desktop. To be clear, it is *not* an application that actually replaces your Operating System desktop in *any* way. It is simply an application (written in Java) that you could(should?) use instead of your native desktop to accomplish MANY of the same things.

Why would you want to use an application instead of just using your native desktop? Answer: To put *you* in control of your desktop.

Not all users will agree but I do not always like when my OS manufacturer "enhances" the native desktop experience with an upgrade. From simple things like "I don't need/want that feature" all the way to "where the hell did my shortcuts go?!". I am a developer and I get VERY accustomed to my desktop experience and rely on it for productivity since I am in front of my computer for at least 8 hours a day. I like having icons for my often used applications on my desktop. I like to have them where I put them and I want them to stay there (even after an OS upgrade). While most of this stems from how Microsoft has upgraded its desktop over the years and I overwhelmingly use Windows both at home and work, another benefit is that I can run this desktop environment on both Mac and Linux AND IT STILL RUNS THE SAME!!!! My icons are the same, they are in the same location, etc. etc.
